Welcome ⅋ Overview

Logistics

  • We will go slow and may not make it through all topics. My goal is for everyone to learn the material at a comfortable pace.
  • The overall goal is to set you up to verify a program of your choice as a final project.
  • All assignments are writing code+proofs in a proof assistant. If your assignment typechecks, you are guaranteed 100% on the assignment.
  • I plan on doing a few problems in class the day the assignment is released.

Proof Assistants

  • A powerful programming language (similar to OCaml)
  • You can also write proofs—these look just like code
  • You can write:
    • Just code
    • Just proofs
    • Proofs about code
    • Code and proofs intertwined (“correct-by-construction”)

Class Outcomes

  • Mathematical proof and logical reasoning
    • Small and simple core foundation
    • Proving = programming
    • You will gain a deep understanding of principles like induction and quantification. This may sound scary, but we will boil these down to a few very simple ideas, and we will exercise them through programming in the proof assistant.
  • The art of specification
    • Necessary and challenging even for testing
    • It's an art
  • The art of verification
    • Much more powerful than testing
    • The spec matters
    • The spec is part of the TCB
    • Some specs which are equivalent in principle may be very different in how easy they are to verify. Some specs are stronger than others. Some specs are less complex than others. You want the simplest possible spec because the spec is trusted—how do you know you got the spec right?
